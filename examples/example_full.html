The area of interest extends 50 km beyond the AO boundary, encompassing HIGHWAY 22 eastbound to the BORDER (grid N7234) and all towns and transportation nodes that could resource enemy reinforcement of OBJ HAMMER within 24 hours.

Annex A

Task Organization

Task Force IRON is organized around three maneuver companies, a weapons company, and task-organized enablers to seize OBJ HAMMER NLT 190500ZAPR26. A Co (ABLE 6) is the decisive operation and main effort throughout Phase II, task-organized with 2d Plt/A/54 BEB (SAPPER 2) OPCON from 181800ZAPR26 for mobility and breach. B Co (BRAVO 6) is a shaping effort, attacks to fix enemy forces on OBJ ANVIL from 190430ZAPR26, and transitions to supporting effort for consolidation. C Co (CHARLIE 6) is the battalion reserve, postured vic. AA EAGLE NK 112 768, prepared to reinforce A Co, conduct counterattack against enemy mechanized reinforcement from the north, or assume A Co's mission on order.

D Co Weapons (DOG 6) provides direct-fire overwatch from SBF 1 (NK 135 776) and SBF 2 (NK 139 777) with TOW and .50 cal sections, priority to A Co Phase I–II then C Co Phase III. Btry B/4-319 AFAR (STEEL 6) is direct support to 2-503 IN from 181200ZAPR26 through 200600ZAPR26; priority of fires shifts in accordance with Annex D. Scout Plt operates TACON to 173 RSTA SQDN through 190430ZAPR26, then chops back to 2-503 IN control as the battalion's organic recon asset in zone.

Main effort is A Co through seizure of OBJ HAMMER; supporting efforts are B Co (fix on OBJ ANVIL) and D Co Weapons (DF support). Reserve is C Co. Unit integrity is preserved at platoon level or higher; no cross-attachments below company without BN CDR approval. All OPCON and DS relationships terminate NLT 200600ZAPR26 unless extended by FRAGO.

Annex B

Intelligence

Enemy on OBJ HAMMER is assessed as a reinforced motorized rifle company (+), approximately 120 pax, with 4x BMP-2, an 82mm mortar section, a ZU-23-2 AAA piece sited for ground role, and Orlan-10 UAS support cueing from regimental level. Annex R

Reports

Required reports are consolidated to minimize administrative load while preserving commander situational awareness. SITREP is submitted every 6 hours on BN CMD net (0000Z, 0600Z, 1200Z, 1800Z) by every company to BN TOC; format per BN TACSOP. SPOTREP is immediate for any PIR trigger, contact, or significant event; flash precedence on BN O/I net in SALUTE format. LOGSTAT is submitted every 8 hours to S-4 on BN ADMIN net; PERSTAT every 6 hours to S-1 on BN ADMIN net.

MEDEVAC requests use the 9-line format on BN MEDEVAC net with launch authority at BN TOC; the 15-line IED/UXO report is used for any unexploded ordnance or IED encounter on BN O/I net. Additional reports: Commander's Critical Information Reports (CCIR) trigger immediate voice report to BN CDR through BN TOC; BN SITREP forward to BCT TOC NLT 30 minutes after each 6-hour cycle.

Report discipline is enforced by the BN TOC battle captain; missed or late reports are logged and addressed at the next BUB. Report formats are published in Appendix 1 and posted at every company CP, BN Main, and CTCP.

SITREP, SPOTREP (SALUTE), LOGSTAT, PERSTAT, 9-line MEDEVAC, 15-line IED/UXO, CCIR. Formats published per BN TACSOP and posted at every CP.
