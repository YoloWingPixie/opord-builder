## 1. (U) Situation

### 1.a. Area of Interest

The area of interest extends 50 km beyond the AO boundary, encompassing HIGHWAY 22 eastbound to the BORDER (grid N7234) and all towns and transportation nodes that could resource enemy reinforcement of OBJ HAMMER within 24 hours.

### 1.b. Area of Operations

AO IRON is bounded by PL STEEL (north), PL BRONZE (south), GRID LINE 23 (west), and HIGHWAY 22 (east). Dominated by rolling mixed forest with cleared agricultural corridors north–south. See Annex C, Appendix 2 (Operation Overlay).

#### 1.b.(1) Terrain (OAKOC)

- **Observation and Fields of Fire:** OBJ HAMMER sits on a 340m ridgeline offering 3–4 km observation east; forest canopy restricts fields of fire to <200m off the ridge.
- **Avenues of Approach:** Two platoon-sized mounted AAs from the south (ROUTE CLUB, ROUTE SPADE); one company-sized dismounted AA along the eastern treeline (ROUTE DIAMOND).
- **Key Terrain:** OBJ HAMMER (MR-342 ridge). Decisive terrain: the eastern spur, grid NK 142 783, which controls observation of ENY fallback positions.
- **Obstacles:** Stream line along PL BRONZE (fordable for dismounts); mined wire obstacle belt templated vicinity NK 138 779 (IPB-confirmed).
- **Cover and Concealment:** Dense coniferous cover throughout the AO; limited cover in the cleared-fields corridor (east-west). Urban cover in VIC ALTENSTADT (NK 115 790).

#### 1.b.(2) Weather

- **Visibility:** 8–10 km; daylight; reducing to 2 km with forecast afternoon precip.
- **Winds:** W 10–15 kt, gusts to 22 kt; favors friendly fires dispersion.
- **Precipitation:** 40% chance intermittent rain 181400Z–182000Z; trafficability degraded on ROUTE CLUB.
- **Cloud Cover:** BKN 4,500 ft; overcast by 181800Z; CAS ceiling remains above 3,000 ft AGL.
- **Temperature and Humidity:** Hi 14°C / Lo 4°C; RH 78%. Cold-weather injury risk LOW.
- **Light Data:** BMNT 0518Z · Sunrise 0558Z · Sunset 1818Z · EENT 1858Z · Moon 62% waxing gibbous

### 1.c. Enemy Forces

**1.c.(1) Composition, Disposition, and Strength.** Motorized rifle company (+) templated on OBJ HAMMER; est. 120 PAX with 4× BMP-2, 2× 82mm mortar, 1× ZU-23 AAA, engineer squad. Company is dug in, two platoons forward, one in reserve.

**1.c.(2) Recent Activities.** Over the past 72 hours, ENY has conducted dismounted patrols to PL BRONZE, emplaced obstacle belt NK 138 779, and rehearsed counterattack from the east using reinforcing elements.

**1.c.(3) Capabilities.** Defend in place; counterattack with a mounted platoon (+) within 2 hrs; disrupt with indirect fires from 82mm mortars out to 4 km; limited UAS (Orlan-10 templated) capability for ISR.

**1.c.(4) Most Likely Course of Action.** ENY defends OBJ HAMMER with two platoons forward, uses obstacle belt and direct fire to canalize friendly attack into EA IRON, then counterattacks with mounted reserve from the east along ROUTE DIAMOND.

**1.c.(5) Most Dangerous Course of Action.** ENY pre-empts the attack with a spoiling raid at 180400Z against friendly assembly area at GRAFENWOEHR (OBJ TUNGSTEN), followed by deliberate defense of OBJ HAMMER and commitment of a reinforcing company (+) from the EAST.

## 3. (U) Execution

### 3.a. Commander's Intent

**3.a.(1) Purpose.** Generate an overmatch of combat power on OBJ HAMMER fast enough to preempt enemy counterattack and hand off the objective to follow-on forces with combat power intact.

**3.a.(2) Key Tasks.**
- Seize OBJ HAMMER with one company decisive, one shaping, one reserve.
- Defeat ENY reserve counterattack forward of PL BRONZE.
- Preserve combat power: less than 10% friendly casualties through consolidation.
- Maintain continuous fires and ISR coverage through transition to follow-on force.

**3.a.(3) End State.**
OBJ HAMMER under TF IRON control by 190900Z; ENY defeated in sector; TF postured for follow-on.

- _Friendly:_ TF IRON consolidated on OBJ HAMMER, >90% combat power, Class V at 70% basic load.
- _Enemy:_ Enemy force on OBJ HAMMER destroyed or forced to withdraw east of HIGHWAY 22.
- _Terrain:_ OBJ HAMMER (MR-342 ridge) and eastern spur under friendly control and observation.
- _Civil:_ No civilian casualties; ALTENSTADT village and cultural sites unaffected.

### 3.b. Concept of Operations

TF IRON executes a three-phase attack: PHASE I (Shaping, 180600Z–190500Z) — reconnaissance and shaping fires; PHASE II (Decisive, 190500Z–190800Z) — dismounted assault on OBJ HAMMER; PHASE III (Consolidate, 190800Z–200600Z) — consolidate and hand off.

**3.b.(1) Maneuver.** A Co conducts the decisive operation as the main effort with dismounted assault along AA DIAMOND. B Co fixes ENY on OBJ ANVIL. C Co is reserve prepared to reinforce A Co or defeat counterattack. D Co provides direct-fire support from SBF-1 (NK 132 778).

**3.b.(2) Decisive Operation.** A Co assaults OBJ HAMMER at 190500Z, seizes the ridge and eastern spur, and consolidates.

**3.b.(3) Shaping Operations.** B Co fixes the ENY platoon on OBJ ANVIL; SAPPER 2 breaches the wire obstacle belt at NK 138 779 NLT 190430Z; STEEL 6 delivers 12-minute artillery preparation on OBJ HAMMER 190448Z–190500Z.

**3.b.(4) Sustaining Operations.** Combat Trains (CTCP) established vicinity NK 118 772 by 190400Z. Ambulance exchange point (AXP) at NK 125 776. CL III/V resupply by LMTV convoy from BSA every 8 hrs.

**3.b.(5) Reserve.** C Co is reserve, held at AA BRONZE, prepared to reinforce A Co (PRIORITY) or block enemy counterattack along ROUTE DIAMOND.

**3.b.(6) Fires.** See Annex D. Priority of fires to A Co during Phase II.

Phase I: B Co conducts supporting attack on OBJ ANVIL starting 180600Z. A Co moves to ASSAULT POSITION SABRE by 190430Z. ### Annex N — Space Operations

Space-based enablers are critical to IRON STRIKE. GPS PNT supports all dismounted and mounted navigation; DAGRs are issued to squad leader and higher. SATCOM (MUOS narrowband, TACSAT) provides BN-to-BCT reachback as the secondary communications path. ISR tipping from national and theater space assets flows through the BCT ACE to BN S-2 within 30 minutes of collection. No offensive space effects are planned or authorized.

The space threat assessment is LOW for IRON STRIKE; however, enemy doctrine includes GPS spoofing and jamming, and the Grafenwoehr training area has historically experienced GPS interference during large exercises. Units carry out pre-mission PNT checks, report any GPS anomaly (drift, sudden fix loss, impossible position) through JSIR on BN O/I net within 15 minutes, and fall back to terrain association and map-and-compass when GPS is degraded.

The BN space officer (dual-hatted with S-6) maintains the running space estimate, submits space support requests through the BCT space support element, and integrates space considerations into the targeting and fires cycle. SATCOM outages, GPS anomalies, and tipping delays are reported immediately to the BN CDR.

**Appendix 1 (N) — Space Support Requests.** BN space officer submits SSRs through BCT SSE for ISR, PNT monitoring, and SATCOM priority. Standing request for MUOS priority during Phase II.
**Appendix 2 (N) — Friendly Space Order of Battle.** Relevant friendly space capabilities: GPS, MUOS, WGS, and national/theater ISR. Integrated view maintained by BCT SSE.
**Appendix 3 (N) — Threat Space Order of Battle.** Enemy GPS jamming/spoofing assessed POSSIBLE; Orlan-10 cueing from regimental space integration. Report anomalies via JSIR within 15 minutes.
